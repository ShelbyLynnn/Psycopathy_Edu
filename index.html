What is Psychopathy?

Everything you need to know

Definition

DSM Definition for PsychopathyThis term was replaced with antisocial personality disorder (ASPD) which focuses on the behavioral aspects of psychopathy, such as aggression, impulsivity, and violations of others’ rights, but only minimally on personality characteristics like callousness, remorselessness, and narcissism.

American Psychological Association. (2022, March). CE Corner: Psychopathy. Monitor on Psychology. Retrieved February 16, 2024, from https://www.apa.org/monitor/2022/03/ce-corner-psychopathy

Plural | Psychopathies : mental disorder especially when marked by egocentric and antisocial activity, a lack of remorse for one's actions, an absence of empathy for others, and often criminal tendencies.

Merriam-Webster. (n.d.). Psychopathy. In Merriam-Webster.com dictionary. Retrieved February 15, 2024 from https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/psychopathy

  • Psychopathy is not a clinical diagnosis, think of it as a spectrum that only certain personalities can be measured by.
  • There is an “agreement” in the psychology field that not every psychopath has the diagnosis of Antisocial Personality Disorder (ASPD). However, it seems forensic psychologists tend to believe otherwise, unfortunately there is lack of evidence to support either side. (1)
